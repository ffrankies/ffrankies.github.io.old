About Me

Hi, my name is Frank (ffrankies is my usual internet pseudonym). Currently, I am a Sophomore studying computer science at Grand Valley State University in Michigan, USA. Where I hail from is somewhat more complicated. I was born in Kharkiv, Ukraine, lived out most of my early childhood in Russia, and then moved to Ghana, the country whose citizenship I now claim. You could say I'm a bit of a global citizen.

I've mentally played around with many careers, from minivan driver (in my defense, I was 4 or 5, and being paid to drive sounded like Heaven on Earth), to a cartoonist, to a singer (that one didn't last long, thankfully), to an engineer. I finally settled on something computer-ey when I realized how much time I spent staring at my screen and thought: "Hey, I might as well do something useful while I'm at it." I've never really looked back since then. There are just so many things you can do with the right coding knowledge; from making websites to automating robots to simulating life (probably).

I would describe myself as a curious person, in the sense that I like to try out new stuff, so long as said 'new stuff' doesn't involve heights or spices. This curiousity is what drove me to try coding the first time, to try different forms of art (like fractals and digital art), to start writing, to download duolingo and start learning Italian, to buy a 'learn to play piano' book and start practicing on my roommate's keyboard... Well, you get the point. I always have at least four different things I'm "trying out", and I wouldn't have it any other way.

As you might have guessed from the above paragraph, I have way too many hobbies, and I periodically jump to and fro between them like some kind of confused rabbit. I am an avid reader (current record is finishing three novels in a single Saturday). Like pretty much every male under 30, I enjoy my video games. I draw, I write, I make periodic attempts to learn Italian. I actively-passively support AC Milan. I'm learning to play the keyboard, I play football (soccer) passably, and tennis (poorly), and I can stand around in ice skates without immediately falling. I rock out to the Beatles, Florence and the Machine, and Michael Jackson, and I think Vikings is the best thing to happen to television since Game of Thrones. Oh, and I code.
